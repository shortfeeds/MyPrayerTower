The family is called the "Domestic Church"—a little church in your home. Here's how to build one.

---

## What Is a Domestic Church?

### The Concept

The Church teaches that the family is a "domestic church"—a place where faith is passed from generation to generation.

### Why It Matters

Your home is where your children will learn their faith (or lack thereof). Make it a place of prayer.

---

## Practical Steps

### 1. Create a Sacred Space

Set aside a corner or room for prayer—a "home altar" with a crucifix, candles, Bible, and prayer books.

### 2. Pray Together

Establish daily prayer—before meals, at bedtime, or both.

### 3. Celebrate the Liturgical Year

Advent, Lent, Easter—make them real in your home. Calendars, traditions, food.

### 4. Go to Mass Together

Make Sunday Mass a non-negotiable family priority.

### 5. Pass on the Faith

Teach your children the faith—prayers, Mass, morality.

---

## A Family Prayer

> *Lord, make our home a Domestic Church. May we pray together, grow together, and love together. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: Your Home Is a Seminary

Your home is where saints are made. Take it seriously.

*Lord, bless our home. Make it a place of prayer. Amen.*
