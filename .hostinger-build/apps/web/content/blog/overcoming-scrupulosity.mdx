Scrupulosity is a form of OCD that focuses on religious or moral concerns. Those who struggle with it feel constant, overwhelming guilt—even when they haven't committed a sin.

Let's explore how to find peace.

---

## What Is Scrupulosity?

### The Symptoms

- Excessive worry about sin
- Fear of having committed a sin
- Repeatedly going to Confession for the same issues
- Inability to accept forgiveness
- Constant examination of conscience

### The Cause

It's often a form of OCD—Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder—where the mind gets stuck on religious fears.

---

## How to Find Peace

### 1. Understand That God Forgives

When God forgives, it's done. You don't need to "feel" forgiven—the forgiveness is real.

### 2. Limit Confession

Don't go to Confession every day. Set boundaries—"I'll go once a month."

### 3. Talk to a Priest

Find a priest who understands scrupulosity. Ask for spiritual direction.

### 4. Get Professional Help

Consider therapy or medication. Scrupulosity often has a neurological component.

### 5. Practice Self-Care

When the urge to confess hits, do something else—exercise, pray, call a friend.

---

## A Prayer for Peace

> *Lord, I know You forgive me. Help me to accept that forgiveness. Free me from the prison of scrupulosity. Give me peace. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: You Can Be Free

Scrupulosity is treatable. With the right help, you can find peace.
