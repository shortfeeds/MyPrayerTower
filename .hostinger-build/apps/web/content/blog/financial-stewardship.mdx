Money is a touchy subject—but the Bible has a lot to say about it. Let's explore.

---

## The Biblical Principle

### Everything Belongs to God

The first principle: It's all God's. We are stewards, not owners.

> "The earth is the Lord's and everything in it" (Psalm 24:1).

### The Tithe

In the Old Testament, the Israelites were commanded to give 10% (a "tithe") to support the temple, the priests, and the poor.

### The New Approach

In the New Testament, we're not bound by law—but the principle remains: we should give generously.

---

## Practical Applications

### 1. Give First

When you receive income, give first—not last. "Tithe first" rather than "tithe what's left."

### 2. Give Joyfully

"God loves a cheerful giver" (2 Corinthians 9:7).

### 3. Give Proportionally

Not everyone can give 10%. But everyone can give something—proportionally.

---

## Common Questions

### Does It Have to Be 10%?

Catholics aren't bound to the exact 10%. But we should give generously—according to our means.

### What If I'm in Debt?

Giving doesn't stop when you're in debt. But prioritize getting out of debt too.
