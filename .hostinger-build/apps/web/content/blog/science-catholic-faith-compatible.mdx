"Are faith and science compatible?" The short answer: Yes. Here's the history and explanation.

---

## The Apparent Conflict

### Galileo

The Galileo controversy is often cited as "proof" of conflict. But it's more complex.

### The Real Story

Galileo taught that the sun (not Earth) was the center—and the Church eventually condemned him. But the issue was as much about authority as science.

---

## Catholic Contributions

### Catholic Scientists

- Gregor Mendel (genetics)
- Nicolas Copernicus (astronomy—Catholic!)
- Georges Lemaître (Big Bang theory—priest!)
- Louis Pasteur (germ theory)

---

## Why They're Compatible

### 1. Same God

Catholics believe God created everything—science and faith have the same Author.

### 2. Different Methods

Science studies the natural; faith studies the supernatural. They don't conflict—they complement.

### 3. Truth Is One

If God is truth, then truth in science and truth in faith must ultimately match.

---

## Conclusion: Harmony

Faith and science aren't enemies—they're friends.
