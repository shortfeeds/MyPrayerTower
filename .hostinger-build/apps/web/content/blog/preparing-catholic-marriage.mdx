You've set a date, booked the church, and completed your Pre-Cana. But there's more to Catholic marriage preparation than a weekend class.

Here's what Pre-Cana doesn't always teach.

---

## The Spiritual Foundation

### 1. Daily Prayer Together

Start praying together *before* the wedding. Before bed, before meals, before your day.

### 2. The Sacrament of Confession

Go to Confession together before the wedding. Start your marriage with a clean slate.

### 3. The Eucharist

Receive Communion together—if you're both Catholic in a state of grace.

---

## The Practical Realities

### 1. Financial Planning

Money is the #1 cause of marital stress. Talk about:
- Debt
- Budgeting
- Spending habits

### 2. Children

Talk about:
- When to have kids
- How many
- How to raise them (Catholic? secular?)

### 3. Household Roles

Who does what? There's no "right" answer—but you need to talk about it.

### 4. In-Laws

Set boundaries now. Know how you'll handle family.

---

## The Hard Conversations

### Past Relationships

Be honest about your past. Don't hide anything.

### Health Issues

Mental health, physical health—share it all.

### Expectations

What do you expect from marriage? From your spouse?

---

## The "First Year" Survival Tips

### 1. Don't Go to Bed Angry

It's cliché—but it matters.

### 2. Prioritize Sex

Make it a priority. Don't let life get in the way.

### 3. Keep Dating

Continue to date after marriage.

### 4. Pray Together

Never stop praying together.

---

## Conclusion: It's a Journey

Marriage isn't a destination—it's a journey. Keep growing together.

*Lord, bless our marriage. Help us to love each other as You love us. Amen.*
