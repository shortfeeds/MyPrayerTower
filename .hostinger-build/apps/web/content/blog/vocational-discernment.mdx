Are you called to religious life, priesthood, or consecrated life? Vocational discernment is the process of discovering God's call.

Here's how to discern.

---

## What Is Vocational Discernment?

Vocational discernment is the process of discovering what God is calling you to—whether marriage, priesthood, religious life, or single life.

---

## Signs of a Call to Religious Life

### 1. Drawn to Prayer

You find yourself drawn to prayer, to the Mass, to the sacraments.

### 2. Service

You have a heart for service—wanting to give your life to others.

### 3. Chastity

You're at peace with the idea of celibacy for the sake of the kingdom.

### 4. Community

You feel drawn to community life.

---

## The Discernment Process

### 1. Pray

Start with prayer. Ask God to reveal His will.

### 2. Talk

Talk to a priest or a spiritual director.

### 3. Visit

Visit monasteries, convents, or seminaries. See what the life is actually like.

### 4. Journal

Keep a journal of your thoughts, feelings, and experiences.

---

## The Different Vocations

### Priests

- Serve the Church through the sacraments
- Preach, teach, lead

### Brothers

- Men religious who don't become priests
- Live in community, serve in various ways

### Sisters/Nuns

- Women religious
- Live in community, serve, pray

### Consecrated Life

- Individuals who consecrate themselves to God
- Various forms

---

## A Prayer for Discernment

> *Lord, I want to know Your will for my life. Reveal to me what You are calling me to. Give me the courage to follow. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: Listen and Respond

God is calling. Are you listening?

*Lord, give me ears to hear Your call. Amen.*
