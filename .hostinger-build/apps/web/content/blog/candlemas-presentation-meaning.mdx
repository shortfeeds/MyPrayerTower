**February 2** is the Feast of the Presentation of the Lord (and the Purification of Mary).
It is traditionally called **Candlemas**.

## The Story (Luke 2)
Mary and Joseph bring baby Jesus to the Temple (40 days after birth).
They meet Simeon, an old man who was promised he wouldn't die until he saw the Messiah.
He takes Jesus in his arms and sings the *Nunc Dimittis*:
*"Lord, now you let your servant go in peace... for my eyes have seen your salvation... a LIGHT for revelation to the Gentiles."*

## The Candles
Because Simeon called Jesus a "Light," we bless candles on this day.
(It is also Groundhog Day... because if the sun shines on Candlemas, winter stays).
