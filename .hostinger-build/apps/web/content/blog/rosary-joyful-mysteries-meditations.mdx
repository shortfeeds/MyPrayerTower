The **Joyful Mysteries** mark the beginning of our redemption. They are prayed on **Mondays** and **Saturdays** (and Sundays during Advent/Christmas). They tell the story of Jesus' "hidden life."

## 1. The Annunciation
**The Event**: Angel Gabriel appears to Mary. She says "Yes" (Fiat).
**The Fruit/Virtue**: **Humility**.
**Meditation**: Mary was troubled, but she trusted God's plan over her own fears.
**Scripture**: *"Behold the handmaid of the Lord; be it done to me according to thy word."* (Luke 1:38)

## 2. The Visitation
**The Event**: Mary travels in haste to help her pregnant cousin Elizabeth. John the Baptist leaps in the womb.
**The Fruit/Virtue**: **Love of Neighbor**.
**Meditation**: Mary carried Jesus to others immediately. She didn't stay home to be pampered; she went to serve.
**Scripture**: *"Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb!"* (Luke 1:42)

## 3. The Nativity
**The Event**: Jesus is born in a stable in Bethlehem.
**The Fruit/Virtue**: **Poverty of Spirit / Detachment**.
**Meditation**: The King of Kings was born on straw. He chose poverty to show us that wealth is not the goal of life.
**Scripture**: *"She wrapped him in swaddling clothes and laid him in a manger."* (Luke 2:7)

## 4. The Presentation
**The Event**: Mary and Joseph bring baby Jesus to the Temple. Simeon prophesies a sword of sorrow.
**The Fruit/Virtue**: **Obedience**.
**Meditation**: They obeyed the Law of Moses, even though they were the Holy Family. They didn't claim exemption.
**Scripture**: *"My eyes have seen your salvation."* (Luke 2:30)

## 5. The Finding in the Temple
**The Event**: 12-year-old Jesus is lost for three days. Found teaching the elders.
**The Fruit/Virtue**: **Joy in Finding Jesus / Piety**.
**Meditation**: Have I lost Jesus through sin? Do I search for Him with sorrow and anxiety until I find Him in the "Temple" (Confession)?
**Scripture**: *"Did you not know that I must be in my Father's house?"* (Luke 2:49)
