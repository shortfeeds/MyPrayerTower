Why does the Catholic Bible have 73 books while the Protestant Bible has 66? Let's explore.

---

## The Difference

### The Catholic Old Testament

Catholics have 46 Old Testament books:

- 39 books (same as Protestants)
- 7 "deuterocanonical" books

### The Protestant Old Testament

Protestants have 39 Old Testament books—no deuterocanonical books.

### The New Testament

Both have 27 books—identical.

---

## What Are the Deuterocanonical Books?

### The 7 Books

1. Tobit
2. Judith
3. Wisdom
4. Sirach (Ecclesiasticus)
5. Baruch
6. 1 Maccabees
7. 2 Maccabees
(Plus parts of Daniel and Esther)

### The History

These books were part of the Greek Septuagint (the Bible used in the early Church). They were included in the Latin Vulgate (St. Jerome's translation).

---

## Why the Difference?

### The Reformation

In the 1500s, Protestant reformers removed these books—mainly because some Protestant leaders didn't consider them authoritative.

### The Council of Trent

In response, the Catholic Church affirmed these books at the Council of Trent (1546).

---

## Conclusion: Same Core

Despite the difference, both Catholic and Protestant Bibles contain the same core message—God's love in Jesus Christ.
