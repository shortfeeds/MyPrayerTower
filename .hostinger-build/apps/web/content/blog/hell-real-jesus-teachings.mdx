Hell is a difficult teaching—but it's part of Catholic doctrine. Let's explore what Jesus taught.

---

## What Jesus Said

### "Enter Through the Narrow Gate"

In Matthew 7:13-14, Jesus says most people take the "broad road to destruction"—but the "narrow gate" leads to life.

### "The Fire Will Never Be Quenched"

In Mark 9:48, Jesus says: "Where their worm does not die, and the fire is not quenched."

### "Depart from Me"

In Matthew 25:41, Jesus says to the wicked: "Depart from me, you cursed, into the eternal fire prepared for the devil and his angels."

---

## What Hell Is

### 1. Eternal Separation

Hell is not primarily about fire—it's about being eternally separated from God.

### 2. The Result of Free Will

God doesn't "send" people to hell—people choose to reject Him.

### 3. Real

The Church has always taught hell is real—not metaphorical.

---

## The Catholic Understanding

### 1. Most People Won't Go

The Church has always believed that most people will be saved—through God's mercy.

### 2. We Don't Know Who's In Hell

We can't say for certain that any specific person is in hell. Only God knows.

### 3. We Should Fear (Appropriately)

We're called to "fear" hell—but as a motivator to avoid sin, not as a terror.
