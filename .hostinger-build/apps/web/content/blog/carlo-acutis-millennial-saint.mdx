In 2020, the Catholic Church canonized its first saint from the millennial generation. Carlo Acutis (1991-2006) was just 15 years old when he died—but his legacy is changing the world.

Let's explore his inspiring story.

---

## The Young Life

### Born in London, Raised in Milan (1991)

Carlo Acutis was born on May 3, 1991, in London, to Italian parents. He grew up in Milan, Italy—an ordinary boy in many ways.

### The Ordinary Teen

Carlo was a normal teenager:
- He loved playing video games
- He was an avid soccer fan (AC Milan)
- He struggled with school (especially math)
- He had a warm relationship with his parents

But he had one extraordinary quality: his deep, deep faith.

---

## The Faith of a Child

### First Communion at Age 9

Carlo received his First Communion at age 9—and it changed him. He became fascinated with the Eucharist.

### The "Eucharistic Miracles" Project

Carlo spent his short life cataloging Eucharistic miracles—documenting over 140 reported miracles of the Eucharist throughout history. He created an exhibit that traveled around the world.

This wasn't homework—it was his passion. He wanted people to know that Jesus is truly present in the Eucharist.

### Daily Mass

Carlo attended Daily Mass as often as he could. He received Communion frequently. His mother said: "For him, the Eucharist was bread for his journey."

---

## The Illness and Death

### The Diagnosis (2006)

In 2006, at age 14, Carlo was diagnosed with acute myeloid leukemia. He suffered greatly—but he never complained.

### His Response

When asked if he was scared, he said: "I offering my suffering to God. If I have to suffer, I want to do it for the Church and for the pope."

He died on October 12, 2006, at age 15.

### His Last Words

His last words to his mother: "Thank you for everything. I'm not afraid, because I am with God."

---

## The Legacy

### The Miracle

A miracle was attributed to his intercession: a child in Brazil with a rare pancreatic condition was healed through his prayers. This cleared the path for his canonization.

### The Canonization (2020)

Pope Francis canonized Carlo Acutis on October 10, 2020. He became the first saint from the millennial generation.

### Why He's Popular with Young People

Carlo is extremely popular with young people because:
- He was one of them
- He loved technology
- He made faith cool
- He shows that holiness is possible for teens

---

## The Lessons of Carlo

### 1. Holiness Is for Everyone

Carlo wasn't a "saint" because he did extraordinary things—he did ordinary things extraordinarily well.

### 2. The Eucharist Changes Everything

Carlo's entire life was oriented around the Eucharist. It's the source of his sanctity.

### 3. Offer It Up

Carlo offered up his suffering for others. He shows us that suffering has meaning.

### 4. Use Your Passions for God

Carlo loved technology and used it to create the Eucharistic exhibit. We can use our gifts for God too.

---

## How to Honor Carlo

### A Prayer to Carlo

> *Saint Carlo Acutis, you who loved the Eucharist so much, help me to understand and receive this great gift. Teach me to offer my daily struggles for others. Obtain for me [intention]. Amen.*

### The "Carlo Challenge"

Some young people make a "Carlo challenge": attend Daily Mass for a week, or create something for God's glory.

---

## Conclusion: A Saint for Our Times

Carlo Acutis proves that you don't need to be old to be holy. You don't need to do extraordinary things. You just need to love—and offer what you have.

*Saint Carlo, pray for us. Amen.*
