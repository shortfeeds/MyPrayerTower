When a friend is grieving, we often feel helpless. We don't know what to say or do. But there are meaningful ways to support them through their journey.

Here's how to help a grieving friend through prayer and faith.

---

## What NOT to Say

Avoid these phrases:
- "They're in a better place" (This can feel dismissive)
- "It was God's will" (Too soon for theology)
- "At least they're not suffering" (Minimizes the loss)
- "You'll find someone else" (For a spouse—way too soon)

---

## What to DO

### 1. Show Up

Don't just offer—do. Show up physically. Be present.

### 2. Listen

Let them talk. Let them cry. Don't try to fix anything.

### 3. Practical Help

- Bring meals
- Help with errands
- Handle practical tasks

### 4. Remember

Remember the important dates—the anniversary, the birthday. Send a note.

---

## Prayers for Their Grief

### A Simple Prayer

> *Lord, be with [name] in their grief. Comfort them. Strengthen them. Heal their heart. Amen.*

### A More Detailed Prayer

> *God of all comfort, You who wipe away every tear, be with my friend [name] in their grief. Give them strength for each day. Help them to trust in Your love. Surround them with people who care. Amen.*

---

## Spiritual Ways to Help

### 1. Have a Mass Offered

Offer a Mass for your friend's intention.

### 2. Pray the Rosary

Dedicate a Rosary for their healing.

### 3. Light a Candle

Light a candle as a sign of your prayer.

### 4. Give a Spiritual Bouquet

Gift them a Spiritual Bouquet—prayers, Masses, and good works offered for them.

---

## Long-Term Support

### Months Later

Grief doesn't end in weeks. Continue to reach out months later.

### The Anniversary

Remember the anniversary. It's a hard day.

### Holidays

Holidays are hard. Check in.

---

## Conclusion: Just Be There

The best thing you can do is just be there. Show up. Pray. Love.

*Lord, help me to be a good friend to those who are grieving. Give me the words to say and the presence to offer. Amen.*
