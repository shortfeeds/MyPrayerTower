Caring for aging parents is one of the most challenging—rewarding—tasks. Here's a Catholic approach.

---

## The Call to Honor

### The Fourth Commandment

"Honor your father and mother" (Exodus 20:12).

### The Vocation

Caregiving is a vocation—a calling from God.

---

## The Spiritual Aspect

### 1. Offer It Up

Offer your caregiving as a sacrifice to God.

### 2. Pray Together

Pray with your parents—simple prayers, the Rosary.

### 3. The Sacramental

Help them receive Confession, Anointing, the Eucharist.

---

## The Practical Aspect

### 1. Plan Ahead

Legal, medical, financial—plan early.

### 2. Accept Help

Don't try to do it all alone. Accept help from siblings, services.

### 3. Care for Yourself

Caregiving is exhausting. Don't neglect your own health.

---

## A Prayer for Caregivers

> *Lord, give me the strength to care for my parents. Help me to honor them as they deserve. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: It's a Privilege

Caring for aging parents is hard—but it's a privilege. One day, you'll wish you could do it again.

*Lord, help me to honor my parents. Amen.*
