The Trinity is the central mystery of Christianity—One God in Three Persons. But it's easy to get wrong. Here's how to understand it.

---

## The Core Doctrine

### One God

There's only one God.

### Three Persons

The one God exists as Three Persons:
- **The Father**
- **The Son (Jesus)**
- **The Holy Spirit**

---

## Common Heresies to Avoid

### 1. Arianism (Denied Christ's Divinity)

"Jesus isn't fully God." WRONG. He's fully God—eternal, co-equal with the Father.

### 2. Modalism (One Person, Different Modes)

"God just plays different roles at different times." WRONG. The Father isn't just the Son pretending. They're distinct Persons.

### 3. Tritheism (Three Gods)

"Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are three separate Gods." WRONG. They're One God—not three Gods.

---

## The Correct Understanding

### The Analogy

- One human nature (nature of being human)
- Three persons (you, me, them)

Similarly:
- One Divine Nature (one God)
- Three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)

### The Definition (The Athanasian Creed)

> "We worship one God in Trinity and Trinity in Unity, neither confounding the persons nor dividing the substance."

---

## Conclusion: Mystery

The Trinity is a mystery—we don't fully understand it. But we believe it.

*Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be. Amen.*
