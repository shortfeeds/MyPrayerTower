Every day, Catholics around the world hear the same readings at Mass. But how do you move beyond just "hearing" to truly *receiving* the Word?

For centuries, the Church has used a method called **Lectio Divina** (Latin for "divine reading")—a slow, prayerful way of engaging Scripture that transforms words into life.

This guide will teach you how to practice Lectio Divina with the daily Mass readings.

---

## What Is Lectio Divina?

Lectio Divina is an ancient monastic practice of praying with Scripture. It's not Bible study—it's *contemplation*. Instead of analyzing the text, we let the text analyze us.

The practice has four steps:
1. **Lectio** (Reading)
2. **Meditatio** (Meditation)
3. **Oratio** (Prayer)
4. **Contemplatio** (Contemplation)

---

## The Four Steps in Detail

### Step 1: Lectio (Reading)

Choose a passage—ideally one of the daily Mass readings. Read it slowly. Read it again. And again.

Don't rush. Let the words sink in. Listen for a word or phrase that catches your attention.

**Practical tip:** Read out loud, if you can. This engages more senses.

---

### Step 2: Meditatio (Meditation)

Now, think about what you've read. Don't just let it wash over you—dwell on it.

Ask questions:
- What is this saying to me?
- What does this say about God? About me?
- Is there a command? A promise? A warning?

**Practical tip:** Write down a word or phrase that stands out. Circle it. Dwell on it.

---

### Step 3: Oratio (Prayer)

Now, respond to God. This isn't formal prayer—it's conversation.

If the text convicts you, confess. If it comforts you, give thanks. If it challenges you, ask for help.

**Practical tip:** Write a prayer in response. Or speak it aloud.

---

### Step 4: Contemplatio (Contemplation)

Finally, rest in God's presence. Stop thinking, stop analyzing, stop praying with words. Just be with Him.

This is the hardest step—and the most rewarding. It's simply *receiving* what God wants to give.

**Practical tip:** Sit in silence. Let God speak to your heart.

---

## How to Apply This to Daily Mass Readings

### Before Mass

1. **Find the readings:** Use a liturgical calendar or app (like MyPrayerTower) to find tomorrow's readings.
2. **Preview them:** Read them once or twice. Don't study—just preview.

### During Mass

1. **Listen for the word:** When a word or phrase catches your ear, mark it in your heart.
2. **Receive the homily:** Pay attention to how the priest explains the readings.

### After Mass

1. **Return to the text:** Review the readings using the four steps above.
2. **Journal:** Write down your thoughts, prayers, and insights.

## A Sample Lectio Divina Session

Let's practice with a common reading: **John 3:16** ("For God so loved the world...")

### Step 1: Lectio

Read the verse slowly. Then read it again. Read it a third time.

### Step 2: Meditatio

Ask: What catches my attention?
- "So loved" — that's intense
- "The world" — that's everyone
- "Give His only Son" — that's everything

### Step 3: Oratio

Pray: "Thank You, God, for loving me so much. I'm overwhelmed by Your gift. Help me to love others the way You love me."

### Step 4: Contemplatio

Rest. Don't think. Just be loved. Let the love sink in.

## Common Questions

### How long does it take?

Start with 15-20 minutes. You can extend as you grow.

### Do I need to use the daily readings?

Not necessarily. Lectio Divina works with any Scripture—but using the daily readings connects your personal prayer to the Church's liturgy.

### What if my mind wanders?

That's normal. Gently bring it back to the text. The wandering is part of the practice.

### What if I "hear" nothing?

Keep going. Sometimes the text speaks quietly. Sometimes you need to come back another day.
