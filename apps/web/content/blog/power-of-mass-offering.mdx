In the Catholic Church, having a Mass offered for someone is considered one of the most powerful spiritual acts available.

But why? And how do you do it?

---

## What Is a Mass Offering?

A Mass offering is the request that a specific Mass be offered for a particular intention—whether for a living person (for their health, their needs, their intentions) or for the dead (for their soul).

The priest celebrates Mass "for" that person or intention.

---

## Why It's Powerful

### 1. The Mass Is Christ Sacrifice

The Mass is not just a "service"—it's the same sacrifice of Christ on the cross, made present on the altar. This sacrifice has infinite value.

### 2. The Priest Acts in Persona Christi

In the Mass, the priest acts "in the person of Christ." When he offers the sacrifice, it's Christ offering Himself.

### 3. It Applies Infinite Grace

The grace of the Mass is infinite. It can be applied to anyone—for their intentions, for their healing, for their salvation.

---

## How to Have a Mass Offered

### 1. Ask Your Parish

Most parishes allow you to request a Mass intention. There's usually a small offering (stipend) required.

### 2. Contact a Priest

If you can't attend your own parish, contact any priest. Most will accept a Mass intention.

### 3. Use an App

At MyPrayerTower, you can request a Mass offering online—a modern way to participate in this ancient tradition.

---

## For Whom Can You Have a Mass Offered?

### For the Living

- For someone's health
- For someone's conversion
- For someone's intentions
- For someone's needs

### For the Dead

- For a soul in Purgatory
- For a deceased loved one
- For all the faithful departed

---

## The Timing

### For the Living

Mass intentions can be for current needs or future events.

### For the Dead

Masses for the dead can be offered at any time—especially on:
- The day of death
- The anniversary of death
- All Souls' Day (November 2)

---

## How Many?

One Mass is powerful. But multiple Masses are even more powerful. Consider:
- A novena (9 Masses)
- A month of Masses
- A year of Masses

---

## A Prayer When Requesting a Mass

> *Lord, I ask that a Mass be offered for [name]. May Your grace and mercy be poured out upon them. May they experience Your love. I offer this sacrifice with love. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: The Most Powerful Gift

When you don't know what else to do—when medicine has failed, when relationships are broken, when all seems lost—have a Mass offered.

It's the most powerful thing you can do.

*Lord, increase my faith. Help me to trust in the power of the Mass. Amen.*
