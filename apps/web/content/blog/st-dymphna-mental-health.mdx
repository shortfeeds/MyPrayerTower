When mental health struggles, there's a powerful saint to invoke: St. Dymphna.

---

## Who Was St. Dymphna?

### The Irish Princess

St. Dymphna (c. 7th century) was an Irish princess who fled an abusive father. She became a nun in Belgium—and was martyred when her father found her.

### The Connection to Mental Health

She became the patroness of those with nervous disorders, mental illness, and anxiety—possibly because of her own suffering or because of the circumstances of her martyrdom.

---

## How to Invoke Her

### The St. Dymphna Prayer

> *Saint Dymphna, patron of those with nervous disorders and mental illness, I come to you for help. Obtain for me the grace to be free from anxiety and fear. Help me to trust in God's love. Obtain for me [intention]. Amen.*

### The Novena

A novena to St. Dymphna is often prayed—nine days of prayer for mental health intentions.

---

## The Shrine

There's a famous shrine in Geel, Belgium, where people have sought healing for centuries.

---

## Conclusion: She's There

St. Dymphna understands. She's waiting to help.

*St. Dymphna, pray for me. Amen.*
