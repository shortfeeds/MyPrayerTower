*"Angel of God, my guardian dear..."*
We learn the prayer at age 5. But do we believe it at age 35?

## The Dogma
The Catechism (CCC 336) says: *"From its beginning until death, human life is surrounded by their watchful care and intercession. Beside each believer stands an angel as protector and shepherd."*
Jesus said: *"See that you do not despise one of these little ones. For I tell you that in heaven their angels always see the face of my Father."* (Matthew 18:10).

## What They DO
1. **Protect**: Physically (from accidents) and spiritually (from demons).
2. **Pray**: They take our prayers to the throne of God (Revelation 8:3).
3. **Prompt**: They suggest good thoughts to our imagination.

## What They DON'T Do
1. **Read your mind**: Only God knows your innermost thoughts. Angels can deduce what you are thinking by your body language, but they can't access your soul directly.
2. **Override your free will**: They can nudge you, but they can't force you to be good.
3. **Become humans**: Humans don't turn into angels when they die. You don't "get your wings." You are a human forever (just a glorified one in heaven).

## Should I Name My Angel?
The Vatican says **No**. We only name the angels God has named (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael). Naming implies authority over something (like Adam naming the animals). We don't have authority over angels; they are superior to us in nature.
