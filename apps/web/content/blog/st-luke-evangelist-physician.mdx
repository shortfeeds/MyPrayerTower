**St. Luke** was a Gentile doctor (Colossians 4:14) and a companion of St. Paul.
He gives us the most detail about Jesus' childhood (Infancy Narratives). Why?
Because he interviewed Mary.

## The Gospel of Mercy
Luke's Gospel focuses on the poor, women, and sinners.
Only Luke tells us the parables of:
- The Good Samaritan
- The Prodigal Son
- Factors of the Lost Sheep/Coin

## The Artist
Legend says Luke was a painter. The famous icon of **Salus Populi Romani** (which Pope Francis visits before every trip) is attributed to him.
Whether he used a brush or not, he certainly painted a portrait of Mary with *words* (The Magnificat).
