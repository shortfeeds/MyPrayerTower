We often think of Mary as the "cause of our joy." But she is also the **Mother of Sorrows** (*Mater Dolorosa*). Her heart was pierced so that ours might be healed.

The devotion to the Seven Sorrows dates back to the Middle Ages but saw a massive resurgence after the apparitions of **Our Lady of Kibeho** in Rwanda (1980s), where Mary asked the world to pray the **Rosary of the Seven Sorrows** to prevent war and harden hearts.

> *"Turn your eyes to your Mother, and see if there is any sorrow like unto my sorrow."*

## The Seven Sorrows
We meditate on each dolor (sorrow) with one [Our Father](/prayers/our-father) and seven [Hail Marys](/prayers/hail-mary).

### 1. The Prophecy of Simeon
At the Presentation, Simeon tells Mary: *"And a sword will pierce your own soul too"* (Luke 2:35). The shadow of the Cross falls over the joy of the birth.

### 2. The Flight into Egypt
Mary and Joseph must flee into the desert to save Jesus from Herod's slaughter. She feels the anxiety of every refugee mother.

### 3. The Loss of the Child Jesus in the Temple
For three days, Jesus is missing. Mary feels the panic of separation from God.

### 4. Mary Meets Jesus on the Way to Calvary
She sees her Son beaten, bloody, and carrying the Cross. Their eyes meet. She cannot help Him; she can only witness His pain.

### 5. The Crucifixion and Death of Jesus
She stands at the foot of the Cross. She watches Him die. She hears His last breath. Her heart breaks, but she stands (*Stabat Mater*).

### 6. Jesus is Taken Down from the Cross
The Pieta. She holds His lifeless body in her arms — the same body she held in the stable at Bethlehem.

### 7. The Burial of Jesus
She lays Him in the tomb and the stone is rolled shut. She must walk away and leave Him in the darkness.

## The Promises
According to St. Bridget of Sweden, Mary made seven promises to those who honor her daily by meditating on her tears and sorrows:

1. I will grant peace to their families.
2. They will be enlightened about the divine Mysteries.
3. I will console them in their pains and I will accompany them in their work.
4. I will give them as much as they ask for as long as it does not oppose the adorable will of my divine Son or the sanctification of their souls.
5. I will defend them in their spiritual battles with the infernal enemy and I will protect them at every instant of their lives.
6. I will visibly help them at the moment of their death—they will see the face of their mother.
7. I have obtained this grace from my divine Son, that those who propagate this devotion to my tears and dolors will be taken directly from this earthly life to eternal happiness, since all their sins will be forgiven and my Son will be their eternal consolation and joy.

## Why Pray It?
- **For Healing**: Mary understands grief, anxiety, and trauma better than anyone.
- **For Peace**: As promised at Kibeho, this prayer has the power to bring peace to troubled hearts and nations.
- **For Insight**: Meditating on the Passion through Mary's eyes reveals depths of Jesus' love you might otherwise miss.
