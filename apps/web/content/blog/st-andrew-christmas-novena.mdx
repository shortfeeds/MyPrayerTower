It is not technically a prayer to St. Andrew.
It just starts on his feast day (**November 30**).
It is a meditation on the exact moment of the Incarnation.

## The Prayer
> *Hail and blessed be the hour and moment in which the Son of God was born of the most pure Virgin Mary, at midnight, in Bethlehem, in piercing cold. In that hour, vouchsafe, O my God! to hear my prayer and grant my desires, through the merits of Our Savior Jesus Christ, and of His Blessed Mother. Amen.*

## The Challenge
Pray it **15 times a day** from Nov 30 to Dec 24.
It sounds like a lot, but it takes about 20 seconds.
- 5 times at breakfast.
- 5 times at lunch.
- 5 times at dinner.

It centers your mind on the "piercing cold" and the warmth of Christ's birth.
