One of the most important Catholic social principles is the "Preferential Option for the Poor." What does it mean?

---

## The Definition

### What It Is

The Preferential Option for the Poor is the teaching that God has a special concern for the poor—and so should we.

### The Principle

Those who are "last" should be "first" in our concern—the marginalized, the vulnerable, the forgotten.

---

## Biblical Basis

### The Old Testament

God repeatedly commands care for the widow, the orphan, the stranger.

### The New Testament

Jesus' ministry focused on the poor: "He has sent me to bring good news to the poor" (Luke 4:18).

---

## How to Live It

### 1. Give

Support the poor—through money, time, advocacy.

### 2. Advocate

Speak up for the marginalized.

### 3. Prioritize

Make the poor a priority in your giving and your time.
