For centuries, she was confused with the "sinful woman" in Luke 7. But the Gospels never say Mary Magdalene was a prostitute. They say Jesus cast **seven demons** out of her.

Whatever her past, her future was glorious.

## Why "Apostle to the Apostles"?
On Easter morning, the men were hiding in fear. Mary was at the tomb.
She was the first to see the Risen Christ.
Jesus gave her a mission: *"Go to my brothers and tell them."*
She was the Apostle (one sent) to the Apostles (those sent). She evangelized the first bishops.

## The Lesson: Persistence
She stayed at the Cross when others fled. She went to the tomb while it was still dark. She wept until she found Him.
She teaches us that **love is stronger than fear**.

## Legend of the Egg
Tradition says she preached to Emperor Tiberius. She held up an egg to explain the Resurrection. He laughed and said, *"A man can no more rise from the dead than that egg can turn red."*
The egg turned bright red in her hand.
This is why we dye Easter eggs.
