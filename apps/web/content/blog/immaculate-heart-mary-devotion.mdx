You rarely see an image of the Sacred Heart without the Immaculate Heart nearby. They beat in rhythm.
While the Sacred Heart is celebrated on a Friday, the **Immaculate Heart of Mary** is celebrated the very next day (Saturday).

## The Symbolism
- **The Sword**: Refers to Simeon's prophecy: *"And a sword will pierce your own soul too"* (Luke 2:35). It represents her shared suffering with Jesus.
- **The Roses**: While Jesus' heart has thorns (sins of men), Mary's has roses. It represents her purity and sweetness.
- **The Fire**: Like her Son, her heart burns with love for God and for us.

## Why Honor Her Heart?
Because it is the **perfect response** to Jesus.
Jesus' heart is the *Source* of grace; Mary's heart is the *Vessel* that received it perfectly. If we want to love Jesus, the best way is to love Him *with* Mary's heart.

## The First Saturday Devotion
Just as Jesus asked for 9 First Fridays, Mary appeared at Fatima and asked for **5 First Saturdays**.
The requirements:
1. Confession.
2. Communion.
3. Rosary.
4. 15-minute meditation on the mysteries.
**Intention**: To make reparation for the blasphemies against her Immaculate Heart.
