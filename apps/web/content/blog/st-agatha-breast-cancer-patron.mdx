**St. Agatha** (d. 251) is one of the 7 women mentioned in the Canon of the Mass.
Like St. Agnes, she was a young virgin who refused a pagan official.
Her torture was gruesome: Her breasts were severed.
Legend says St. Peter appeared to her in prison and healed her wounds before she was finally martyred.

## Patronage
She is the patron of:
- Breast Cancer patients.
- Rape victims.
- Wet nurses.
- Bell-founders (due to the shape of severed breasts... medieval humor was dark).
She stands as a witness that the body can be destroyed, but the soul belongs to God alone.
