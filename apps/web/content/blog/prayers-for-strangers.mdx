In the Catholic Church, we're all connected—even to strangers we've never met. Here's why your prayers for them matter.

---

## The Communion of Saints

### What It Is

The Communion of Saints is the teaching that we're all connected—living and dead, known and unknown.

### The Unity

We're one body in Christ. What affects one, affects all.

---

## Why Praying for Strangers Matters

### 1. They're Part of the Body

Strangers are part of God's family too. They need prayers.

### 2. Our Prayers Are Powerful

"the prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective" (James 5:16).

### 3. We're Connected

We don't know who needs our prayers—but God does.

---

## How to Pray for Strangers

### 1. The Prayer Wall

Post prayer intentions—and let others pray for them. This is the communion of saints in action.

### 2. General Intercession

At Mass, we pray for "the whole world"—not just our loved ones.

### 3. Daily Intentions

Each day, add a stranger to your prayers.

---

## A Prayer for Strangers

> *Lord, I pray for [name] today—whom I may never meet. Watch over them. Bless them. Help them. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: We're All Connected

The Communion of Saints is real. Our prayers for strangers do matter.

*Lord, help me to pray for others. Amen.*
