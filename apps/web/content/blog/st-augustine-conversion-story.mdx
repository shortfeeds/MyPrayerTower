**St. Augustine of Hippo** (354-430) is proof that no one is too far gone.

## The Man
He was a genius. He was a rhetorician. He was cool.
He was also lost. He spent his 20s searching for truth in Manichaeism (a gnostic cult) and pleasure in the arms of a mistress.
But he was miserable. *"You have made us for Yourself, O Lord, and our heart is restless until it rests in Thee."*

## The Garden (Tolle Lege)
One day in a garden in Milan, he was weeping over his inability to change. He heard a child's voice singing: *"Tolle, lege. Tolle, lege"* (Take up and read).
He opened a Bible to Romans 13:13: *"Not in orgies and drunkenness, not in sexual immorality and debauchery... but clothe yourselves with the Lord Jesus Christ."*

The light of confidence flooded his heart. The chains of lust were broken.

## The Doctor
He went on to become the Bishop of Hippo and wrote *The City of God* and *Confessions*. He single-handedly shaped Western theology on Grace, Original Sin, and the Trinity.
