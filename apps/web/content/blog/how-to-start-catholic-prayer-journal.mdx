*"I remember praying for the things I have now."*

If you don't write it down, you will forget. You will forget the answered prayers, the small graces, and the moments of clarity. A prayer journal acts as a "memorial stone" (Joshua 4:7) of God's faithfulness in your life.

Here are 3 simple ways to start, even if you hate writing.

## Method 1: The A.C.T.S. Ledger
Divided your page into 4 quadrants. This keeps it short and structured.
- **A (Adoration)**: "God, You are..." (e.g., "Faithful even when I am tired")
- **C (Confession)**: "I am sorry for..." (e.g., "Snapping at my husband")
- **T (Thanksgiving)**: "Thank you for..." (e.g., "The sun, coffee, the rent being paid")
- **S (Supplication)**: "Please help..." (e.g., "My sister's surgery")

## Method 2: Scripture Response (Lectio Divina)
Write down **one verse** from the daily Gospel. Then write 2-3 sentences of what God is saying to *you* through it.
*Example:*
> **Verse**: "Cast your nets on the other side." (John 21:6)
> **Response**: Lord, I feel like I've been working so hard with no results. You are asking me to trust You and try a new way, even if it seems foolish. Help me to let go of my own control.

## Method 3: The "Letters to God" (Freestyle)
Just write "Dear Jesus," and dump your brain. Tell Him your anger, your fear, your confusion. Be raw. He can handle it.
*Pro Tip*: End every entry with: *"Jesus, I trust in You."*

## Why Journal?
1. **Focus**: It stops your mind from wandering. The pen keeps you tethered to the thought.
2. **History**: Reading back over old journals is the best way to see how much you've grown (and how faithfully God has answered prayers you forgot about).
3. **Release**: Getting worries out of your head and onto paper engages a different part of the brain ("externalization"), reducing anxiety.

## FAQ

### Is it a sin if I write something angry at God?
No! Look at the Psalms. King David yelled at God constantly (*"Why have you forsaken me?"*). God wants your honesty, not your politeness.

### Do I have to do it every day?
No. Start with Sundays, or whenever you feel overwhelmed.

### Digital or Analog?
Pen and paper are best because they slow you down and have no notifications. But a notes app works if that's all you have.
