We celebrated his birth in June. Now (August 29), we remember his death.

**St. John the Baptist** is the patron saint of speaking truth to power.
He didn't die for preaching Jesus. He died for **marriage**.
He told King Herod: *"It is not lawful for you to have your brother's wife."*

## The Party
Herod threw a birthday bash. His step-daughter (Salome) danced for him. It pleased him so much (read: he was drunk and lustful) that he promised her anything.
Her mother (Herodias) whispered: *"The head of John the Baptist."*

## The Plate
It is one of the most grotesque scenes in the Bible. A holy head on a platter at a banquet.
John was silenced. But his witness echoed forever.

## The Lesson
The world hates moral truth. If you stand up for marriage, for life, or for purity, you will lose your "head" (reputation, job, friends).
John says: **Do it anyway.**
