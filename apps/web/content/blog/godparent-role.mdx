If you've been asked to be a godparent (or "sponsor"), you might wonder: What exactly am I supposed to do?

Let's explore the role.

---

## The Definition

A godparent (or sponsor) is someone who presents a person being baptized and affirms their faith alongside the parents.

In the early Church, the sponsor was responsible for teaching the catechumen (person being instructed) about the faith.

---

## The Requirements

To be a godparent, you must:
- Be Catholic
- Be baptized (and confirmed, if Catholic)
- Be at least 16 years old
- Be in a state of grace (not have committed a serious sin)
- Not be the parent of the person being baptized

---

## The Responsibilities

### 1. Present the Person

At Baptism, you present the person to the priest—"I present [name] for Holy Baptism."

### 2. Affirm the Faith

You answer questions about your faith, affirming that you believe what the Church believes.

### 3. Pray

Your primary job is to pray for your godchild—daily, if possible.

### 4. Be an Example

Live your Catholic faith in a way that inspires your godchild.

### 5. Help Form

If the parents fail, you're responsible to help form the child in the faith.

---

## A Prayer for Your Godchild

> *God, I present to You [name]. Watch over them. Protect them. Draw them to Yourself. Give me the grace to be a good godparent. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: A Lifetime Role

Being a godparent isn't just for the day of Baptism—it's a lifetime role.

*Lord, help me to be a faithful godparent. Amen.*
