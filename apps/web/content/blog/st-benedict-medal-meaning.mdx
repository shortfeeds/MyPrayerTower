When the Roman Empire was collapsing, one man decided to rebuild society from the ground up.
**St. Benedict of Nursia** (480-547) is the Patron Saint of Europe for a reason. His monks preserved literacy, agriculture, and order during the Dark Ages.

## The Rule: Ora et Labora
His secret was balance. The "Rule of St. Benedict" is built on two pillars:
1. **Ora (Prayer)**: The monks pray the Psalms 7 times a day (Liturgy of the Hours).
2. **Labora (Work)**: Idleness is the enemy of the soul. Monks work in the fields, copy manuscripts, and brew beer.

## The Medal
The **St. Benedict Medal** is one of the most powerful sacramentals in the Church. It is basically an exorcism in metal.
The letters on the back stand for a Latin prayer:
- **C S S M L**: *Crux Sacra Sit Mihi Lux* (May the Holy Cross be my light).
- **N D S M D**: *Non Draco Sit Mihi Dux* (May the dragon never be my guide).
- **V R S N S M V**: *Vade Retro Satana* (Begone Satan).
- **S M Q L I V B**: *Sunt Mala Quae Libas* (The things you offer are evil).
- **I V B**: *Ipse Venena Bibas* (Drink your own poison).

## The Poisoned Cup
Why the "poison" reference? Because his own monks once tried to poison him (he was too strict). He blessed the cup, and it shattered.
