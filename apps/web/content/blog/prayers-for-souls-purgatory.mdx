In Catholic teaching, we have a duty to pray for the dead—especially the souls in Purgatory who need our prayers to be purified before entering heaven.

Let's explore this important tradition.

---

## Why We Pray for the Dead

### The Communion of Saints

The Church teaches that we're all connected—living and dead. We're one family in Christ.

### They Need Our Prayers

The souls in Purgatory are already saved—but they need purification. Our prayers help speed that process.

### It's a Work of Mercy

Praying for the dead is one of the "Spiritual Works of Mercy."

---

## The Traditional Prayers

### 1. The Eternal Rest (Dies Irae)

> *Eternal rest, grant unto them, O Lord. And let the perpetual light shine upon them. May they rest in peace. Amen.*

### 2. The De Profundis (Psalm 130)

> *Out of the depths I have cried to You, O Lord: Lord, hear my voice. Let Your ears be attentive to the voice of my supplication. If You, O Lord, should mark iniquities: Lord, who could stand? But there is forgiveness with You: that You may be revered.*

### 3. The Rosary

Praying a Rosary for the dead is especially powerful.

### 4. The Divine Mercy Chaplet

The Divine Mercy Chaplet can also be offered for the dead.

---

## How to Pray

### 1. Daily

Make it a habit: "Today I'll pray for the souls in Purgatory."

### 2. For Specific Intentions

When someone you love dies, commit to praying for their soul.

### 3. On All Souls' Day

November 2 is especially set aside for praying for the dead.

---

## The Benefits

### For Them

Our prayers help release souls from Purgatory. Every prayer matters.

### For Us

Praying for the dead reminds us of our own mortality—and helps us live better.

---

## Conclusion: Don't Forget Them

The dead are not forgotten. We pray for them. We remember them. And one day, they'll pray for us.

*Lord, help me to remember the dead. May my prayers help release the souls in Purgatory. Amen.*
