Summer is for slowing down. It is the perfect time to tackle that book you've been meaning to read.
Here are our top picks for 2026.

## 1. Fiction: *Kristin Lavransdatter* (Sigrid Undset)
**Genre**: Historical Fiction / Epic
**Why**: Nobel Prize winner. Set in medieval Norway. It follows the life of Kristin — her sins, her motherhood, her redemption. It is the most "Catholic" novel ever written. Warning: You will cry.

## 2. Spirituality: *The Introduction to the Devout Life* (St. Francis de Sales)
**Genre**: Classic
**Why**: Written specifically for lay people living in the world (not monks). Short chapters. Perfect for reading 10 minutes a day by the pool.

## 3. Biography: *With God in Russia* (Fr. Walter Ciszek)
**Genre**: Thriller / Memoir
**Why**: An American Jesuit spy captured by the Soviets and sent to the Siberian Gulag for 23 years. A gripping story of faith under pressure.

## 4. Modern: *StrangeGods* (Elizabeth Scalia)
**Genre**: Cultural Commentary
**Why**: An analysis of the idols we worship today (politics, technology, coolness). Very relevant for 2026.

## 5. Classic: *Brideshead Revisited* (Evelyn Waugh)
**Genre**: Literary Fiction
**Why**: A story of a broken, wealthy British family and the "twitch upon the thread" of God's grace that pulls them back before the end.
