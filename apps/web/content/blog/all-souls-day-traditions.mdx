November 2 is All Souls' Day—a day set aside to pray for all the faithful departed, especially those in Purgatory.

Let's explore the traditions and prayers of this important day.

---

## What Is All Souls' Day?

### The Date

All Souls' Day is November 2—immediately after All Saints' Day (November 1).

### The Purpose

The day is dedicated to prayer for the souls in Purgatory—all the faithful departed who need purification before entering heaven.

---

## The Traditions

### 1. The Mass

The central tradition is the Mass—offered for all the souls in Purgatory.

### 2. Visiting the Cemetery

Many Catholics visit the graves of their loved ones on this day, cleaning and decorating the tombstones.

### 3. Lighting Candles

Candles are lit throughout the church—as a sign of prayer for the dead.

### 4. The "Eternal Rest" Prayer

The traditional prayer is said throughout the day:

> *Eternal rest, grant unto them, O Lord. And let the perpetual light shine upon them. May they rest in peace. Amen.*

---

## How to Participate

### At Mass

Attend Mass on November 2. Offer your intentions for the dead.

### At the Cemetery

Visit the graves of your loved ones. Clean them. Leave flowers. Pray.

### At Home

Light a candle. Say a prayer. Remember.

## Prayers for All Souls' Day

### The De Profundis (Psalm 130)

> *Out of the depths I have cried to You, O Lord: Lord, hear my voice.*

### The Litany for the Dead

A special litany is often prayed on this day.

### The Rosary

A Rosary offered for the dead is especially powerful.

---

## A Personal Prayer

> *Lord, today I remember all the faithful departed—those I knew and those I didn't. I ask for Your mercy upon them. May they soon rest in peace. I offer my prayers for their release. Amen.*

---

## Conclusion: A Day of Memory

All Souls' Day is a day to remember. We remember those who have gone before us. We pray for their release. And we remember that we, too, will one day die.

*Lord, give rest to the souls in Purgatory. May they soon be with You in heaven. Amen.*
