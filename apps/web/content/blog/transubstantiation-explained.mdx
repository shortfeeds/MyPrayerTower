Transubstantiation is one of the most distinctive—and debated—Catholic teachings. But what exactly is it?

---

## What Is Transubstantiation?

### The Definition

Transubstantiation is the change of the *substance* of bread and wine into the *substance* of Christ's Body and Blood.

The *accidents* (what we see, taste, touch) remain bread and wine. But the underlying *substance* (the "whatness") becomes Christ.

---

## The Explanation

### Substance vs. Accidents

- **Accidents:** What we perceive with our senses—appearance, taste, texture
- **Substance:** The underlying reality—what something *is*

In transubstantiation, the accidents remain, but the substance changes.

---

## Biblical Basis

### The Words of Institution

At the Last Supper, Jesus said: "This is my body... This is my blood."

He didn't say: "This represents my body." He said: "This *is* my body."

---

## Why It Matters

### It's Not Symbolism

It's not a "symbol" or "reminder." It's the *real presence*.

### It's a Mystery

We don't fully understand it. But we trust God's power to do what He says.

---

## Conclusion: Believe It

Transubstantiation is a hard teaching—but it's Catholic teaching.
