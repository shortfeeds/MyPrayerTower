The Rosary is the most beloved prayer in Catholic devotion — a meditation on the life of Christ through the eyes of His Mother, Mary. Popes, saints, and ordinary Catholics for over 800 years have turned to the Rosary in times of joy, sorrow, and everything in between.

If you have never prayed the Rosary — or if you tried and felt lost — this guide will walk you through every step, every prayer, and every mystery. By the end, you will have everything you need to begin.

> *"The Rosary is the most excellent form of prayer and the most efficacious means of attaining eternal life."* — **Pope Leo XIII**

## What You Need

- **A Rosary** — A set of beads with a crucifix, or you can use your fingers
- **15–20 minutes** — A full Rosary (5 decades) takes about 15 minutes
- **A willing heart** — That's it. You don't need to be perfect

## The Structure of the Rosary

The Rosary consists of:
1. **Opening Prayers** (Crucifix + first beads)
2. **5 Decades** — Each decade includes 1 [Our Father](/prayers/our-father), 10 [Hail Marys](/prayers/hail-mary), and 1 [Glory Be](/prayers/glory-be)
3. **A Mystery** to meditate on during each decade
4. **Closing Prayers**

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Make the Sign of the Cross
Hold the crucifix and say:
> *In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.*

### Step 2: The Apostles' Creed (on the Crucifix)
> *I believe in God, the Father Almighty, Creator of heaven and earth; and in Jesus Christ, His only Son, our Lord; who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary; suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died, and was buried. He descended into hell; the third day He rose again from the dead; He ascended into heaven, is seated at the right hand of God the Father Almighty; from thence He shall come to judge the living and the dead. I believe in the Holy Spirit, the holy Catholic Church, the communion of Saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and life everlasting. Amen.*

### Step 3: Our Father (First Large Bead)
> *Our Father, who art in heaven, hallowed be Thy name. Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done, on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread, and forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.*

### Step 4: Three Hail Marys (Three Small Beads)
Pray three [Hail Marys](/prayers/hail-mary) for an increase in Faith, Hope, and Charity:
> *Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee. Blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus. Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death. Amen.*

### Step 5: Glory Be
> *Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit. As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.*

### Step 6: Announce the First Mystery and Pray the Decade
1. **Announce the Mystery** — State the Mystery you will meditate on (see below)
2. **Our Father** — On the large bead
3. **10 Hail Marys** — On the 10 small beads, while meditating on the Mystery
4. **Glory Be** — After the 10th Hail Mary
5. **Fatima Prayer** (optional but widely prayed):
> *O my Jesus, forgive us our sins, save us from the fires of hell, and lead all souls to heaven, especially those in most need of Thy mercy. Amen.*

### Step 7: Repeat for All 5 Decades
Move through the rosary beads, praying each decade with a different Mystery.

### Step 8: Closing Prayers
After the 5th decade, pray the [Hail Holy Queen](/prayers/hail-holy-queen):
> *Hail, Holy Queen, Mother of mercy, our life, our sweetness, and our hope. To thee do we cry, poor banished children of Eve; to thee do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears. Turn then, most gracious Advocate, thine eyes of mercy toward us. And after this our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of thy womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary. Pray for us, O holy Mother of God, that we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ. Amen.*

Finish with the Sign of the Cross.

## The 20 Mysteries of the Rosary

### The Joyful Mysteries (Monday & Saturday)
1. **The Annunciation** — The Angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will bear the Son of God (Luke 1:26-38)
2. **The Visitation** — Mary visits her cousin Elizabeth (Luke 1:39-56)
3. **The Nativity** — Jesus is born in Bethlehem (Luke 2:1-20)
4. **The Presentation** — Jesus is presented at the Temple (Luke 2:22-40)
5. **The Finding in the Temple** — Mary and Joseph find the child Jesus teaching in the Temple (Luke 2:41-52)

### The Luminous Mysteries (Thursday)
1. **The Baptism of Jesus** — Jesus is baptized in the Jordan River (Matthew 3:13-17)
2. **The Wedding at Cana** — Jesus performs His first miracle at Mary's request (John 2:1-12)
3. **The Proclamation of the Kingdom** — Jesus preaches repentance and the coming of the Kingdom (Mark 1:14-15)
4. **The Transfiguration** — Jesus is transfigured on Mount Tabor (Matthew 17:1-8)
5. **The Institution of the Eucharist** — Jesus gives us His Body and Blood at the Last Supper (Matthew 26:26-28)

### The Sorrowful Mysteries (Tuesday & Friday)
1. **The Agony in the Garden** — Jesus prays in Gethsemane (Luke 22:39-46)
2. **The Scourging at the Pillar** — Jesus is brutally whipped (John 19:1)
3. **The Crowning with Thorns** — Soldiers mock Jesus with a crown of thorns (Matthew 27:29)
4. **The Carrying of the Cross** — Jesus carries His cross to Calvary (John 19:17)
5. **The Crucifixion** — Jesus dies on the Cross for our sins (John 19:18-30)

### The Glorious Mysteries (Wednesday & Sunday)
1. **The Resurrection** — Jesus rises from the dead (Matthew 28:1-10)
2. **The Ascension** — Jesus ascends into heaven (Acts 1:9-11)
3. **The Descent of the Holy Spirit** — The Holy Spirit descends at Pentecost (Acts 2:1-4)
4. **The Assumption of Mary** — Mary is taken body and soul into heaven
5. **The Coronation of Mary** — Mary is crowned Queen of Heaven and Earth

## Tips for Building a Rosary Habit

1. **Start with one decade** — You don't have to pray all five at once. One decade takes 3 minutes.
2. **Pray at the same time daily** — Attach it to a habit you already have (morning coffee, commute, before bed)
3. **Use the [MyPrayerTower app](/)** — Set daily Rosary reminders
4. **Don't worry about perfection** — If your mind wanders, gently bring it back. God honors the effort.
5. **Pray with your family** — "The family that prays the Rosary together stays together"
6. **Listen to audio Rosaries** — Many apps and YouTube channels guide you through each decade
7. **Carry a Rosary** — In your pocket, your purse, your car. Having it nearby removes the barrier to praying

## FAQ

### How long does it take to pray the Rosary?
A full 5-decade Rosary takes about **15–20 minutes**. A single decade takes about 3 minutes. You can pray as much or as little as your time allows.

### Can I pray the Rosary without beads?
Yes. You can count on your fingers, use a Rosary ring, or simply keep mental track. The beads are a helpful tool, but they are not required.

### Which mysteries do I pray on which day?
- **Monday & Saturday** — Joyful Mysteries
- **Tuesday & Friday** — Sorrowful Mysteries
- **Wednesday & Sunday** — Glorious Mysteries
- **Thursday** — Luminous Mysteries

### Is the Rosary in the Bible?
While the Rosary as a devotion was formalized in the Middle Ages, its prayers are deeply biblical. The [Our Father](/prayers/our-father) comes from Matthew 6:9-13. The [Hail Mary](/prayers/hail-mary) comes from Luke 1:28 and Luke 1:42. The mysteries are all rooted in Scripture.

### Can non-Catholics pray the Rosary?
Absolutely. The Rosary is a meditation on the life of Jesus Christ. Anyone who wishes to pray it is welcome.
