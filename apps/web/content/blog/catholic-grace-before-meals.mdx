Praying before eating is the most basic Catholic habit. It reminds us that our food is a gift, not a right.

## The Standard Grace (Before Meals)
> *Bless us, O Lord, and these Thy gifts, which we are about to receive from Thy bounty, through Christ our Lord. Amen.*

## Grace After Meals (The Forgotten Prayer)
We allow the Jews and the Muslims to outdo us here! They pray *after* eating to thank God for satisfaction. Catholics have a prayer for this too:

> *We give Thee thanks, Almighty God, for all Thy benefits, who livest and reignest, world without end. Amen.*
> *May the souls of the faithful departed, through the mercy of God, rest in peace. Amen.*

## Seasonal Variations

### Advent
> *Come, Lord Jesus, be our guest, and let these gifts to us be blest. Prepare our hearts to receive You. Amen.*

### Easter
> *Christ is risen, Alleluia! Bless this food and our fellowship as we celebrate His victory. Amen.*

### Latin (Benedic, Domine)
> *Benedic, Domine, nos et haec tua dona, quae de tua largitate sumus sumpturi. Per Christum Dominum nostrum. Amen.*
