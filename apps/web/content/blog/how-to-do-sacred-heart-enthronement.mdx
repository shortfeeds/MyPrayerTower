In many homes, the TV is king. In others, the smartphone rules.
The **Enthronement of the Sacred Heart** is a conscious decision to say: *"No. In this house, Jesus is King."*

It is more than just hanging a picture. It is a covenant.

## What You Need
1. An **image or statue** of the Sacred Heart of Jesus.
2. An image of the Immaculate Heart of Mary (optional but recommended).
3. A priest (ideal) or the head of the household (father/mother) to lead.
4. Holy Water.

## The Ceremony Steps

### 1. Preparation
Place the image in a place of honor (living room mantle or main wall). Decorate with flowers and candles.

### 2. The Blessing
If a priest is present, he blesses the image. If not, sprinkle it with Holy Water.

### 3. The Enthronement
The head of the household places the image in its permanent spot.
**Leader**: *"We welcome you, Lord Jesus, into our home and into our hearts. Be the King of this family."*

### 4. The Consecration Prayer
Recite the Act of Consecration together:
> *O Sacred Heart of Jesus... we choose You as the sole Ruler and King of our home. We consecrate to You our souls, our bodies, our possessions, and our lives...*

### 5. Signing the Certificate
It is traditional to sign a certificate with the date and the names of all family members. Frame it near the image.

## Living It Out
The Enthronement is not a one-time event.
- **Renew the consecration** every year on the Feast of the Sacred Heart.
- **Greet Jesus** when you leave and enter the house.
- **Make decisions** asking: *"What would the King want us to do?"*
