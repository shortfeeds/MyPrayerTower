St. Ambrose once told her: *"It is impossible that the son of so many tears should perish."*

He was right. But it took 17 years.

## The Difficult Life
**St. Monica** (332-387) had a trifecta of problems:
1. Her husband (Patricius) was a pagan with a violent temper.
2. Her mother-in-law lived with them and hated her.
3. Her son (Augustine) was brilliant but lived a life of sin (he had a mistress and joined a cult).

## Her Strategy
She didn't nag. She didn't yell. She **stormed heaven**.
She followed Augustine from Africa to Rome to Milan. She fasted. She wept. She begged bishops to talk to him.

## The Victory
Before she died in Ostia, she stood with Augustine looking out a window. He was now a baptized Catholic.
She said: *"Son, nothing in this world now affords me delight. I do not know what there is now for me to do or why I am here, now that all my hopes in this world are accomplished."*
She died 9 days later. Her job was done.
