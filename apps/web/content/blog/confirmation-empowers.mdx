Confirmation is often seen as "the Catholic graduation"—but it's much more than that. It's the sacrament that empowers us to be witnesses for Christ.

Let's explore.

---

## What Is Confirmation?

### The Definition

Confirmation is the sacrament that completes Baptism—in which we receive the fullness of the Holy Spirit.

### The Symbols

- The bishop (or priest) lays hands on us
- We are anointed with chrism (holy oil)
- We're marked as soldiers of Christ

---

## The Effects

### 1. The Holy Spirit Comes

We receive the Holy Spirit in a new way—empowering us for mission.

### 2. We're Marked

We're marked forever—a seal of belonging to Christ.

### 3. We're Empowered

We're given the grace to defend the faith, to witness, to live boldly.

---

## Living It Out

### 1. Be a Witness

Don't be afraid to share your faith.

### 2. Defend Truth

When the faith is attacked, speak up.

### 3. Serve

Use your gifts in service of the Church.

---

## Conclusion: A Call to Mission

Confirmation isn't the end—it's the beginning. You're now sent.

*Lord, pour out Your Holy Spirit upon me. Empower me to be a witness. Amen.*
