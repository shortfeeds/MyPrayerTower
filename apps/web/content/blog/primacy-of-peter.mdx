The Papacy is one of the most distinctive Catholic teachings. But what does the Bible say? Let's explore.

---

## The Key Passage: Matthew 16:18-19

> *And I tell you, you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of hell will not prevail against it. I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven, and whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.*

---

## What It Means

### "You Are Peter"

"Peter" (Petros) means "rock." Jesus is changing Simon's name to "Rock."

### "On This Rock"

Jesus says He'll build His church "on this rock." The "rock" is Peter (and his successors).

### "Keys of the Kingdom"

The "keys" symbolize authority. Peter is given authority to "bind and loose"—to teach and govern.

---

## Other Biblical Evidence

### Peter's Leadership in the Early Church

- Peter is first in the list of apostles (Matthew 10:2)
- Peter speaks first at Pentecost (Acts 2)
- Peter leads the early Church (Galatians 2:11)

### Jesus Prays for Peter

> "I have prayed for you that your faith may not fail" (Luke 22:32)

---

## The Catholic Understanding

The Pope (as Peter's successor) is the visible head of the Church on earth—continuing Peter's authority.

---

## Conclusion: A Unique Office

The Papacy is unique in Christianity. Catholics believe it's Christ's design—the way He preserves unity in the Church.
