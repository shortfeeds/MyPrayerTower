St. Dominic (1170-1221) was a man on fire.
While St. Francis was embracing poverty in Italy, Dominic was in France fighting the **Albigensian heresy** (a cult that believed the physical world was evil).

## The Hound
His mother had a dream before he was born: She gave birth to a dog with a torch in its mouth, setting the world on fire.
Today, Dominicans are called *Domini Canes* (Dogs of the Lord). They bark the truth to protect the sheep.

## The Rosary Legend
Dominic was discouraged. The heretics weren't listening. He went into a forest to pray.
Tradition says Mary appeared to him and gave him the **Rosary** as a weapon.
She said: *"Preach my Psalter (150 Hail Marys) and you will win souls."*
Whether physically or spiritually, the Rosary *did* become the primary tool of the Dominicans. They used it to meditate on the Incarnation (Jesus taking on flesh), which arguably destroyed the heresy that flesh was evil.

## The Legacy
The Order of Preachers (O.P.) gave us St. Thomas Aquinas, St. Catherine of Siena, and St. Martin de Porres.
