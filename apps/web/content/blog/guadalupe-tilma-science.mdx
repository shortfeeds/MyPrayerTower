St. Juan Diego was a 57-year-old Aztec convert. Mary appeared to him on Tepeyac Hill and asked him to pick roses in December.
He carried them in his *tilma* (cloak) to the Bishop. When he opened it, the roses fell out, and the image of the Virgin was printed on the fabric.

## The Impossible Science
1. **The Fabric**: Cactus fiber usually disintegrates in 20 years. This tilma is 500 years old and pristine.
2. **The Paint**: Nobel Prize winner Richard Kuhn analyzed fibers and found no animal, vegetable, or mineral pigment. It is not "painted." The image hovers above the fabric.
3. **The Eyes**: Ophthalmologists found microscopic images reflected in her eyes (the Bishop, Juan Diego, and others) following the laws of Purkinje-Sanson curvature. Human eyes reflect; paintings don't.
4. **The Temperature**: The fabric maintains a constant temperature of 98.6°F (human body temp).

## The Result
In 10 years, 9 million Aztecs converted to Catholicism. It was the greatest mass conversion in history.
