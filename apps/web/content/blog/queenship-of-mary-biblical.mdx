In the Davidic Kingdom (Old Testament Israel), the King had many wives. So, who was the Queen?
It wasn't the wife. It was the **Mother**.
She was called the *Gebirah* (Great Lady).

## The Evidence (1 Kings 2:19)
When Bathsheba (the Queen Mother) enters the throne room regarding Solomon:
1. The King stands up to meet her.
2. He bows to her.
3. He places a throne for her at his **right hand**.
4. He says: *"Ask, Mother, for I will not refuse you."*

## The Fulfillment
Jesus is the new Solomon. He is the Davidic King.
Therefore, His mother (Mary) is the new *Gebirah*.
She is not a Queen in her own right; she is Queen *because* she is the Mother of the King.

## Revelation 12
*"A great sign appeared in heaven: a woman clothed with the sun, with the moon under her feet, and on her head a crown of twelve stars."*
There is only one woman crowned in heaven.
