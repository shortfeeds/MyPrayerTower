We treat St. Anthony like a cosmic Google search engine. *"Tony, Tony, look around. Something's lost and must be found."*
But St. Anthony of Padua (1195-1231) was not a soft, sentimental figure. He was a powerhouse preacher who converted thousands and was known as the **Hammer of Heretics**.

## The Story
Born in Portugal, he joined the Augustinians but switched to the Franciscans because he wanted to be a martyr in Morocco. Illness forced him back to Italy, where he met St. Francis of Assisi.
His preaching was so electrifying that shops closed when he came to town.

## The Miracles
1. **Preaching to Fish**: When heretics in Rimini refused to listen to him, he went to the river and preached to the fish. Thousands of fish raised their heads above the water to listen. The heretics were converted.
2. **The Donkey and the Eucharist**: A heretic challenged him to prove the Real Presence. He starved his donkey for three days, then offered it food or the Eucharist. The donkey ignored the food and knelt before the Monstrance.
3. **The Lost Psalter**: A novice stole Anthony's psalter (prayer book). Anthony prayed. The novice saw a terrifying vision and returned it. This is why he is the patron of lost items.

## Prayer for Lost Things
> *O blessed St. Anthony, the grace of God has made you a powerful advocate in all our needs and the patron for the restoring of things lost or stolen. I turn to you today with childlike love and deep confidence. You have helped countless children of God to find the things they have lost, material things, and, more importantly, the things of the spirit: faith, hope, and love.*
