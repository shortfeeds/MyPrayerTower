40 days after Easter, Jesus took His disciples to the Mount of Olives. He blessed them, and then *"he was taken up before their very eyes, and a cloud hid him from their sight"* (Acts 1:9).

This is the **Feast of the Ascension**.

It seems like a sad day. Jesus is leaving! The Apostles are alone again! But the Scriptures say they returned to Jerusalem *"with great joy"* (Luke 24:52). Why?

## 1. He Is Opening the Door
Before the Ascension, heaven was "closed" to humanity. By ascending, Jesus (who is fully human and fully God) brings **human nature** into the presence of the Father for the first time.
> *"He ascended not to distance Himself from our lowly state but that we, his members, might be confident of following where he, our Head and Founder, has gone before."* — **Preface of the Ascension**

Basically: Where the Head (Jesus) goes, the Body (Us) will follow. He is scouting the trail.

## 2. He Is Sending the Spirit
Jesus told the Apostles: *"It is for your good that I am going away. Unless I go away, the Advocate will not come to you"* (John 16:7).
Jesus' physical presence was limited to one place at one time. His **Sacramental presence** (through the Holy Spirit) is available everywhere, to everyone, at all times. He left so He could be closer to us.

## 3. He Is Enthroned as King
The Ascension is Jesus' coronation. He sits at the "right hand of the Father." He is not just a teacher or a prophet; He is the **King of the Universe**.

## FAQ

### Is it a Holy Day of Obligation?
In many places (including the Province of Boston, Hartford, New York, Newark, Omaha, and Philadelphia), **yes**, it is celebrated on Thursday.
However, in many other U.S. dioceses, the celebration is **transferred to the following Sunday** to make it easier for the faithful to attend. Check your local parish bulletin!

### What is the "cloud"?
The cloud is not just weather. In the Bible, a cloud symbolizes the **Shekinah Glory** — the presence of God (like the cloud on Mt. Sinai or at the Transfiguration). Entering the cloud means entering the mystery of God.
