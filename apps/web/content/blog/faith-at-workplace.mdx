You want to share your faith at work—but you don't want to make coworkers uncomfortable or damage relationships.

Here's how to be a witness without alienating anyone.

---

## The Balance

### 1. Live First, Talk Second

Before you speak, live your faith. Be known as a good employee—honest, reliable, kind.

### 2. Don't Preach

No one likes a coworker who preaches. Instead, let your life speak.

### 3. Answer When Asked

If someone asks about your faith, answer honestly—but don't lecture.

### 4. Respect Boundaries

Not everyone wants to hear about your faith. Respect that.

---

## Practical Tips

### 1. Use Common Ground

Find common values—family, honesty, integrity. Connect on those.

### 2. Invite, Don't Impose

Invite coworkers to church or a parish event—but don't pressure.

### 3. Be Private When Appropriate

Some conversations belong outside work. Know when to stop.

### 4. Pray at Your Desk

If appropriate, have a crucifix or image. Prayer is private, powerful.

---

## What to Avoid

- Preaching at work
- Using faith to judge others
- Being "holier than thou"
- Making coworkers uncomfortable

---

## Conclusion: Be the Light

Let your light shine—but gently.

*Lord, use me at my workplace. Amen.*
