We love novenas. But do you know where the idea of a "9-day prayer" comes from?

It comes from the Bible. Specifically, it comes from the **9 days between Ascension Thursday and Pentecost Sunday**.

Before Jesus ascended into heaven, He told His disciples: *"Do not leave Jerusalem, but wait for the gift my Father promised"* (Acts 1:4). So, Mary and the Apostles went to the Upper Room and prayed constantly for nine days. On the tenth day, the **Holy Spirit** fell as tongues of fire.

This is the **Pentecost Novena** — the mother of all novenas.

## Why Pray It?
We often pray to the Father and the Son, but we neglect the Holy Spirit. Yet the Spirit is the "Lord and Giver of Life." Without Him, our faith is dry, intellectual, and powerless.
Praying this novena is asking for a **personal Pentecost**.

## How to Pray It
**Start Date**: Friday after Ascension Thursday (10 days before Pentecost).
**End Date**: Pentecost Eve.

### Daily Themes
Each day focuses on one of the **7 Gifts of the Holy Spirit**:
- **Day 1**: Charity
- **Day 2**: Joy
- **Day 3**: Peace
- **Day 4**: Patience
- **Day 5**: Kindness
- **Day 6**: Faithfulness
- **Day 7**: Gentleness
- **Day 8**: Self-Control
- **Day 9**: Goodness

### The Prayer: "Veni Creator Spiritus"
A classic hymn to use daily:

> *Come, Holy Spirit, Creator blest,*
> *and in our souls take up Thy rest;*
> *come with Thy grace and heavenly aid*
> *to fill the hearts which Thou hast made.*

## The Effects
St. Josemaria Escriva said: *"We must not be content with speaking to the Paraclete, listening to him, and asking him to help us. We must do everything we can to make him feel welcome in our soul."*

When you pray this novena, you are cleaning the house of your soul so the Divine Guest can move in.
